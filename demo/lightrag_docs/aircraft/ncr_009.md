# NCR-2024-0467: Engine Fan Blade FOD Damage — Bird Strike Event

**Report ID:** NCR-2024-0467  
**Date:** 2024-09-18  
**System:** Propulsion / Engine  
**Severity:** Critical  
**Supplier:** N/A (In-service event)  
**Part Number:** P/N CFM56-5B-FAN-01  
**Aircraft:** Airbus A321-231, Tail LZ-FBJ  
**Engineer:** Yannick Morel  

## Defect Description
Bird strike event at approximately 800ft AGL during initial climb from Runway 28R. 
Post-landing inspection revealed impact damage to CFM56-5B/3 engine No. 2 fan section. 
Four consecutive fan blades (positions 14–17 of 36) exhibited leading-edge damage: 
blades 14 and 15 with notching up to 18mm depth, blades 16 and 17 with chord-wise 
cracks extending 9mm from leading edge. Engine continued to operate normally 
following the event with no thrust loss detected by EEC.

## Root Cause
Bird strike confirmed by biological residue analysis (pigeon, Columba livia, 
estimated mass 0.4 kg). Fan blade titanium alloy (Ti-6Al-4V) damage consistent 
with medium bird ingestion at climb speed approximately 180 KIAS. Blade geometry 
at positions 14–17 is in the zone of highest blade chord width, making these 
positions statistically most susceptible to single-bird impact accumulation. 
No manufacturing defect identified — damage classified as in-service FOD event.

## Corrective Action
1. Remove and replace fan blades at positions 14–17 (P/N CFM56-5B-FAN-01)
2. Fan rotor balance check and trim balance as required per AMM 72-21-11
3. Borescope inspection of fan case liner, LPT inlet guide vanes, and combustor
4. Engine performance test run to verify EGT margin and fuel flow within limits
5. Submit bird strike report to EASA and national aviation authority per regulatory requirement
